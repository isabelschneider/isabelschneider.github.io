​​About Me:​​

Isabel is a visual artist, curator, teacher, art conservation technician, and museum development coordinator.

She holds a B.F.A. from the University of Georgia and has worked in New York, Israel, Morocco, and Italy. In 2016,she trained at the International Conservation Center in Acre, Israel (in associate with ICCOMOS and UNESCO), and worked as a technician for the Fine Arts Conservation Department of the Israel Antiquities Authority in Jerusalem. Currently, she serves at the development coordinator for the Breman Museum in Atlanta, GA.

In her art, she uses a variety of mediums, from wood sculpture to encaustics, but she primarily focuses in aqueous media and installation sculpture. Her work explores human relationships to and through geography.
